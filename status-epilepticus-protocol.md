Status Epilepticus – Emergency and ICU Management

Definition

Status epilepticus (SE) is a neurological emergency characterized by:

Continuous seizure activity ≥ 5 minutes

Two or more seizures without recovery of consciousness between them


Prolonged seizures can lead to neuronal injury, metabolic derangement, and systemic complications, requiring immediate treatment.


Classification

1. Convulsive Status Epilepticus (CSE)

Generalized tonic-clonic seizures

Most common and life-threatening form


2. Non-Convulsive Status Epilepticus (NCSE)

Altered mental status

Subtle motor signs

Requires EEG for diagnosis


3. Focal Status Epilepticus

Focal motor activity

May progress to generalized seizures

Initial Emergency Assessment

Airway

Ensure airway patency.

Breathing

Provide oxygen and monitor respiratory effort.

Circulation

Monitor BP, pulse, IV access.

Disability

Assess GCS and seizure activity.

Exposure

Look for trauma, infection, toxidromes.

Toxicology screen

Immediate Treatment

Step 1 – Benzodiazepine (First Line)

Lorazepam

Dose:

0.1 mg/kg IV (maximum 4 mg)

May repeat once after 5–10 minutes.

Alternative:

Diazepam

0.15–0.2 mg/kg IV

or

Midazolam

10 mg IM

if IV access unavailable.

Step 2 – Second Line Antiepileptic

Administer if seizure persists after benzodiazepine.

Levetiracetam

60 mg/kg IV
Maximum 4500 mg

Valproate

40 mg/kg IV
Maximum 3000 mg

Phenytoin / Fosphenytoin

20 mg/kg IV

Step 3 – Refractory Status Epilepticus

Defined as seizures persisting after benzodiazepine + second line AED.

Requires ICU admission.

Options
Midazolam Infusion
Loading:
0.2 mg/kg IV
Maintenance:
0.05–2 mg/kg/hr

Propofol Infusion
Loading:
1–2 mg/kg
Maintenance:
20–200 mcg/kg/min

Thiopental
Used in refractory cases.

Super Refractory Status Epilepticus
Seizures persist >24 hours despite anesthesia.

Management options:
Ketamine infusion
Inhalational anesthetics
Hypothermia
Ketogenic diet
Immunotherapy

Surgical options

ICU Management :
Continuous EEG Monitoring
Essential for detecting non-convulsive seizures.
Ventilation
Mechanical ventilation may be required.

Hemodynamic Monitoring
Monitor BP, heart rate.

Metabolic Correction
Correct
Hypoglycemia
Electrolyte abnormalities
Acid–base disturbances

Complications >>
Neurological:
Neuronal injury
Cognitive impairment
Persistent epilepsy

Systemic:
Aspiration pneumonia
Rhabdomyolysis
Acute kidney injury
Lactic acidosis
Hypotension

Prognostic Factors >>
Poor prognosis associated with:
Delayed treatment
Elderly age
Structural brain disease
Refractory status epilepticus
Prolonged seizure duration

Key Emergency Algorithm -
1. Stabilize ABC
2. Check blood glucose
3. Give benzodiazepine
4. Administer second-line AED
5. If persistent → ICU and anesthetic infusion
6. Continuous EEG monitoring

Key Clinical Pearls
Treat seizures after 5 minutes
Early benzodiazepine reduces mortality
Always check glucose
Do not delay second-line AED
Consider NCSE in unexplained coma
