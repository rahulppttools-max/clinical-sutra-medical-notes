Quick reference for commonly used critical care medications, vasopressors, sedation drugs, and emergency drugs.


Vasopressors

Noradrenaline (Norepinephrine)

Indication

Septic shock

Vasodilatory shock


Dose

0.05 – 1 mcg/kg/min IV infusion

Dilution

4 mg in 50 ml NS

Target

Maintain MAP ≥ 65 mmHg


---

Vasopressin

Indication

Adjunct therapy in septic shock


Dose

0.03 units/min IV infusion

Usually added when norepinephrine requirement increases.


---

Adrenaline (Epinephrine)

Indications

Cardiac arrest

Anaphylaxis

Refractory shock


Infusion dose

0.05 – 0.5 mcg/kg/min


---

Dopamine

Dose

5 – 20 mcg/kg/min

Used less frequently now due to arrhythmia risk.


---

Sedation Drugs

Midazolam

Bolus

0.02 – 0.1 mg/kg IV

Infusion

0.05 – 0.2 mg/kg/hr


---

Propofol

Loading

1 – 2 mg/kg IV

Maintenance

20 – 200 mcg/kg/min infusion

Monitor for hypotension and Propofol Infusion Syndrome.


---

Dexmedetomidine

Dose

0.2 – 0.7 mcg/kg/hr infusion

Useful for light sedation and ventilated patients.


---

Analgesia

Fentanyl

Bolus

1 – 2 mcg/kg IV

Infusion

1 – 3 mcg/kg/hr

Preferred analgesic in ventilated ICU patients.


---

Morphine

Dose

2 – 5 mg IV bolus

Repeat as required.


---

Neuromuscular Blockers

Atracurium

Bolus

0.5 mg/kg IV

Infusion

5 – 10 mcg/kg/min

Commonly used in ARDS ventilation.


---

Rocuronium

Intubation dose

1 – 1.2 mg/kg IV


---

Rapid Sequence Intubation (RSI) Drugs

Induction Agents

Etomidate

0.2 – 0.3 mg/kg IV

Hemodynamically stable.


---

Ketamine

1 – 2 mg/kg IV

Preferred in hypotensive patients.


---

Propofol

1 – 2 mg/kg IV

Avoid in hypotension.


---

Antiepileptic Drugs

Lorazepam

0.1 mg/kg IV
Maximum 4 mg

First-line for seizures.


---

Levetiracetam

60 mg/kg IV loading
Maximum 4500 mg


---

Valproate

20 – 40 mg/kg IV loading


---

Anticoagulation

Enoxaparin

40 mg SC once daily

Renal impairment

30 mg SC once daily


---

Stress Ulcer Prophylaxis

Pantoprazole

40 mg IV once daily


---

Electrolyte Correction

Potassium

10 – 20 mEq/hour IV infusion

Monitor ECG.


---

Magnesium Sulfate

2 g IV over 10 minutes

Used in torsades de pointes.


---

Emergency Drugs

Adrenaline Cardiac Arrest

1 mg IV every 3–5 minutes


---

Atropine

0.5 mg IV every 3–5 minutes
Maximum 3 mg


---

ICU Clinical Pearls

Always titrate vasopressors according to MAP

Monitor renal function and electrolytes

Use infusion pumps for all critical drugs

Avoid oversedation

Daily sedation interruption recommended
